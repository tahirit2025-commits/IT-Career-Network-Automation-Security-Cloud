Telecom & Service Provider Networking

English Section

This directory provides a structured, academic-oriented overview of Telecommunications and Service Provider Networking. The content is designed to support university-level study and practical skill-building for individuals pursuing careers in networking, telecom engineering, or ISP operations.


---

1. Introduction

Telecommunications and Service Provider Networking form the backbone of modern digital communication. From mobile networks to internet service delivery, these systems rely on complex infrastructures, protocols, and operational models. This folder aims to present these concepts in a clear, academically grounded manner while maintaining practical relevance.

The material is suitable for:

University students studying IT, Computer Science, or Telecommunications

Entry-level engineers preparing for ISP roles

Learners seeking structured foundational knowledge



---

2. Objectives

This section aims to enable learners to:

Understand the essential components of telecom infrastructure

Explain how ISPs build, manage, and scale their networks

Analyze routing and switching technologies used in carrier-grade environments

Understand transport technologies such as fiber optics and microwave systems

Develop a foundational understanding of mobile network architecture from 2G to 5G

Apply theoretical concepts to real-world service provider scenarios



---

3. Core Topics Covered

3.1 Fundamentals of Telecommunications

Principles of signal transmission

Analog vs digital communication

Multiplexing techniques (TDM, FDM)

Bandwidth, latency, and capacity considerations


3.2 ISP Network Architecture

Access, distribution, and core layers

Peering and transit relationships

Role of IXP (Internet Exchange Points)

Network scalability principles


3.3 Routing in Service Provider Environments

Interior Gateway Protocols (OSPF, IS-IS)

Exterior Gateway Protocol (BGP) and policy control

Traffic engineering concepts

MPLS and label switching fundamentals


3.4 Transport Systems and Fiber Optics

Fiber types and physical properties

DWDM and CWDM technologies

Optical amplifiers and signal regeneration


3.5 Wireless and Microwave Communication

Line-of-sight requirements

Frequency planning

Microwave link budgeting

Typical ISP wireless deployments


3.6 Mobile Networks (2G–5G)

Core network vs radio access network (RAN)

Components such as MSC, SGSN, MME, HSS, AMF

Packet core evolution (GPRS → EPC → 5G Core)

Principles of handover, mobility management, and QoS


3.7 Monitoring, Diagnostics, and NOC Operations

Fault detection and incident response

KPI measurement and analysis

Traffic monitoring and capacity planning

Tools used in professional NOC environments
